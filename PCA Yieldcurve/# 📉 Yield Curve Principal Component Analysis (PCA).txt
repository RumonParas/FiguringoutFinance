# 📉 Yield Curve Principal Component Analysis (PCA)
Unsupervised Learning for Fixed-Income Risk Management

## 📌 Project Overview
This project applies Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to the US Treasury Yield Curve to solve the pervasive issue of multicollinearity in fixed-income modeling. By compressing 11 highly correlated bond maturities into 3 uncorrelated mathematical factors, the model successfully captures ~88% of the total variance in the bond market over the last decade.

## 🧠 Economic Rationale & Feature Engineering
When modeling macroeconomic variables or pricing fixed-income derivatives, feeding a machine learning model the entire yield curve (1-Month to 30-Year) causes mathematical instability because the bonds move in tandem. 

 The Data Pipeline Fetched 10 years of pristine daily US Treasury yields from the Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED) API.
 Transformations Converted absolute yields into daily basis-point changes to model the movement of the curve, followed by standard scaling (Z-scores) to ensure short-term volatility didn't outweigh long-term trends.
 Dimensionality Reduction Deployed `scikit-learn`'s PCA engine to extract the underlying, invisible macroeconomic forces driving the market.

## 🔬 The Factor Loadings (Empirical Macroeconomics)
The algorithm, completely blind to economic theory, mathematically deduced the three fundamental laws of fixed-income trading

1. PC1 (The Level - 65.1% Variance) A parallel shift metric. Empirically proves that while the Federal Reserve anchors the 1-Month rate via policy, inflation expectations cause the 2-Year through 30-Year maturities to float uniformly.
2. PC2 (The Steepness - 14.4% Variance) A curve-slope metric pivoting at the 5-Year mark. Accurately models economic cycle expectations (FlattenerSteepener regimes).
3. PC3 (The Curvature - 8.9% Variance) A mid-curve convexity metric. Models medium-term supplydemand dislocations in the belly of the curve, enabling Butterfly Spread trading strategies.

## 📊 Business Application Institutional Risk Management
This model serves as the foundational layer for an institutional risk engine
 Factor Sensitivities Replaces traditional 11-bucket Key Rate Duration with 3 pure PCA factor exposures.
 Stress Testing Enables Chief Risk Officers to simulate historical scenarios (e.g., Apply a 3-Sigma shock to PC2 to simulate a sudden 2008-style yield curve steepening).
 Hedging Allows portfolio managers to isolate and hedge against structural inflation (PC1) without neutralizing their alpha bets on the economic cycle (PC2).
